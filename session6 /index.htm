system: is an interconnected set of elements that is coherently organized in a way that achieves something must consist of three things: elements, interconnections, a function or purpose ex. Digestive system: teeth, enzymes, stomach, intestines interrelated though physical flow of food, and though a set of regulating chemical signals function of this system is to break down food into its basic nutrients, transfer into bloodstream (another system), while discarding unusable wastes Systems can be imbedded in systems Is anything not a system? Yes, a conglomeration without any particular interconnections or functions arbitrarily add or take away pieces of a system—ends up no longer being the same system when something dies, it loses its “system-ness” the multiple interrelations that held it together no longer function, although its material remains part of a larger food-web system Systems can chage, adapt, respond to events, seek goals, mend injuries (although they may contain or consist of nonliving things) new, never before imagined systems can arrive out of one system
